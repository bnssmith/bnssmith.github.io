Job Market Paper

Did Medicaid Fuel the Early Opioid Epidemic? An Investigation Using State Policies

Abstract: I explore whether increasing access to Medicaid contributed to the early opioid epidemic, between 1996 and 2010. To address endogeneity in Medicaid eligibility, I use a novel collection of data sources to generate simulated instrumental variables as a measure of state eligibility policies for non-elderly adults without disabilities, I find that pharmaceutical opioid sales and opioid abuse treatment admissions increased by approximately 0.72\% and 3.21\%, respectively, for every 1p.p. increase in the fraction of individuals eligible for Medicaid in a given state. Effects on opioid overdose deaths are statistically insignificant, suggesting that any increase in opioid abuse due to the increase in sales was offset by other effects of Medicaid, including the increase in abuse treatments. Each estimated effect is more negative in larger states than in smaller states, suggesting meaningful interactions between Medicaid policies and state regulations. I discuss possible takeaways and areas for future research.

The Impact of COVID-19 on Emerging Markets Economiesâ€™ Financial Conditions

With Shaghil Ahmed, Jasper Hoek, Steve Kamin, and Emre Yoldas

Abstract: We explore the role of COVID-19 indicators in changing financial variables among Emerging Market Economies (EMEs). Using panel regressions, we find that lockdown stringency has the effects of depreciating currencies, widening credit spreads, and pushing down equity prices in a sample of 22 EMEs. These results underscore the broader dangers posed by the COVID-19 pandemic to already vulnerable countries.
