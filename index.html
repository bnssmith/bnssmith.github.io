Research

Job Market Paper

Did Medicaid Finance the Early Opioid Epidemic? An Investigation Using State Policies

Abstract: I explore whether expanding access to Medicaid contributed to the opioid epidemic’s First Wave (between 1996 and 2010), when pharmaceutical drugs dominated growth in the market for opioids. Applying a simulated instrumental variable strategy to a novel collection of data sources, I estimate same-year effects of plausibly exogenous increases in state-level Medicaid eligibility rates for adults under 65 years old. Pharmaceutical opioid sales and opioid abuse treatment admissions increased by approximately 3.5% and 15.8%, respectively, for every 1p.p. increase in a given state’s enrollment rate measure. Effects on opioid overdose deaths are positive and comparable in magnitude to the effect on opioid sales, but the estimated coefficient is statistically insignificant. I discuss possible mechanisms and areas for future research.
